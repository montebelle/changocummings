Chango Cummings

Founder and Principal

Changó Cummings is an interdisciplinary Architectural designer/Artist who lives and works between NYC/Minneapolis, USA. Changó’s practice exists at the intersection of art, design, placemaking and regenerative community building. His experience is rooted in the local community, having worked for over 10 years in Minneapolis/NYC art and community spaces. Changó uses his practice as a catalyst for social movement, focusing his efforts on the Black American experience. He sees Black spaces as not only a lived and breathed experience but also a making process. Much of Chango’s work lives within the public realm, including buildings, transit corridors, parks, plazas and in between spaces. Chango’s major exhibitions/achievements include: “ITS Factory, POD Sculpture” Minneapolis, MN 2021, “These Streets Weren’t Made For Tenderness” Parsons School of Design MFA Fine Arts Show, featuring works “Anti-surveillance: THOBE” “Haven in The Voids: NODE.” New York, NY (Fall 2018), “THROUGH THE GLASS EYES” Northern Spark Arts grant for public art installation, Minneapolis, MN (2018), “Resilience in Rondo” artist grant for Roots of Rondo, Saint Paul MN (2016), “ITS [Intro to Summer] Arts Festival” group exhibition, Gallery 13, Minneapolis, MN (Summer 2016), “Swift Series” project URBANFRAME MIT at MIT Cambridge, MA (Summer 2015), “North Arrow” 20-foot stainless steel sculpture installed at HUB building (2014), “Constructive Criticism” group exhibition at Burnet Gallery, Chambers Hotel (2008), “Banners Over Broadway” Public art collaboration with David Choe (2007).

Chango is a graduate of Parsons School of Design’s Bachelors in Architectural Design program. He is currently working as an independent Artist/Designer, developing his design and research Thesis Hattiesburg House. Hattiesburg House is the redesign and rebuild of the southern Black American home. He is using the project as a way to record oral and written southern, farming and building histories. Changó is also teaching and taking freelance projects, and fellowships.
